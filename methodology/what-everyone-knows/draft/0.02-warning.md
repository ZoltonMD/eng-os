## Warning

There will be many different approaches, principles and methodologies down below. I know that that may be difficult, but please do not try to apply them all at once. So many changes at once may be overwhelming. Your brain may need all its resources to control the changes instead of doing the work. And as a result all these changes may become a hindrance rather than a help. We cannot afford this. Before you start changing anything, you must learn one very simple but crucial rule. All our planning, analyzing, and reflecting on the work done should take up only 5% of our time. The remaining 95% of our time is dedicated to doing. 

You must understand, It took me years to learn how to use all of this and find a suitable configuration for me. I just did it as we do it every day working with any closed or complicated system. I changed only one incoming parameter and waited for the result. Then, if the result is positive, I was “marinating the change in production”. I applied this approach to my daily work and saw if it would stick. If you change multiple parameters, you will never know which of them impacted the output.

One more key thing to understand from the start: productivity isn't about working longer hours. It's about getting greater results in the same amount of time. Maximizing output, not maximizing input.

Be careful, let it time, and do not overwhelm yourself.
