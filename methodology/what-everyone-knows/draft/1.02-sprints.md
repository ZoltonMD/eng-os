## II. Sprints

Have you run a marathon? I think it's one of those things which is not necessary to try yourself to understand that it's very difficult and very demanding. It's **What Everyone Knows**. Most people can't do it physically (me, for example). To finish a marathon you must be in good shape, have good health, and be properly trained. But if we take a look at your entire running history, and sum all segments you ever ran, what will we get? How many marathons have we run in our lives? Even without special preparations and training, every month we cover huge distances moving with short segments every day. It's a very simple logic and it works in many areas of your life. 

Of course it's not the same as a real marathon. But from the point of view of the work done it's the same work that is done for the same time. And sometimes even more, because you start every new chunk full of energy and motivation.
